    Celebrate TEN YEARS of Majora's Mask

    WindFish
    October 2010 edited October 2010 in Games Galore Quote
    The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask, frequently called many players' favorite Zelda game of all time, was released ten years ago tomorrow in North America.

    There's all the great moments in that game. The melancholy atmosphere. The fatalistic tone. The haunting music.

    It's about making people happy, even if we know we're going to die. About healing our pain. Then you get a stamp in your Bomber's notebook.

    I remember reuniting the girl and her dad in the Ikana Valley music box. And the first time a Garo pulled a knife on me in Ikana. There's Tingle who was weird but not yet creepy. The epic Anju and Kafei quest that was satisfying to finally put together. Saving the ranch from aliens and helping the sisters stay connected.

    The music in Clock Town changes day-to-day to match the impending doom. You learned the routines of the people's lives.

    The fucking moon and it's creepy face. And making it to the observatory to catch a tear dropping off of it.

    Dekus and monkeys. Gorons and babysitting. Zora rock guitars and pirate Gerudo ninjas! More undead ninjas and skeleton kings and holy shit the temple just flipped upside down!

    Then there's the technical improvements over Ocarina of Time. Better draw distance, textures, motion blur.

    People complained at the time about the save system and that you are stuck as kid Link the whole time. The lack of a "suspend-anywhere" is inconvenient, but the three-day cycle made a very dense and deep game with a quick turnaround from Ocarina resources.
